ValidateIt - A Real-World Blockchain Project

ValidateIt is a Project for my Blockchain & Cryptocurrencies Class under Prof. Jothi K. R. This page outlines a few aspects of the same. ValidateIt leverages the power of blockchain technology to provide secure and tamper-proof Certificate Verification and Creation services.
With ValidateIt, universities can easily create digital certificates that can be verified by anyone using blockchain technology. This ensures that the certificates are authentic and cannot be altered or tampered with in any way.

AIM

Managing student transcripts is a painful and document-heavy process that incurs costs on educational institutions and locks students away from accessing, proving, and transferring their credits. Hence, the aim of this project is to create/build a data-centric system that issues digital transcripts via the W3C Verifiable Credential standard.

OBJECTIVE

To create such a blockchain system, we must briefly understand a few terminologies. As explained in the aim of the project, the need for this data centric system is to ensure tamper evident and efficient verification of student documents by authorised personnel. Instead of paper work, it will be easier to verify digital documents called Verifiable Credentials issued to students by their universities. Such a VC will help students prove specific rights depending on the issuer, ie, the university. For example, the university offers you a diploma in the form of this VC for your DID (decentralised identifier) as a student after viewing your national ID or some other legal document. This can be verified, the transaction will be stored on a public ledger and then signed by the private key of your universiity. The objective of the project is to create this public ledger to store information for everyone to independently verify the credentials.

SCOPE

The scope of this project starts with anyone from any domain and their university, and extends to valid employers and other educational institutes they wish to join in. In the above example, the student receives the VC from their university, goes to the employer who verifies it, and then considers the student for a job.
